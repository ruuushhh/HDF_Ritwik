Most often there are misconceptions, stigma, stereotypes, misinformation and even prejudice associated with certain marginalised communities in our society. Individuals tend to make judgements and act in terms of these when communicating with each other. Additionally, individuals belonging to these communities face tremendous amounts of mental health and emotional concerns. There is a lack of self-understanding and ways of coping with the emotional disturbances that are associated with the prejudice. Project MEHAK is an initiative undertaken by the Healing Dove Foundation and ICHARS (Institute of Clinical Hypnosis and Related Sciences) Support Foundation to initiate a dialogue with different members of marginalised communities, to help create mental health awareness and acceptance for all, and to discuss ways of coping with mental health and emotional concerns. The main aim is to equip all in dealing with issues faced in everyday life.
Project Mehak
Aims and objectives
Increasing awareness about the diverse communities
Breaking myths with regard to prevalent stereotypes
Reducing stigma associated with belonging to specific groups
Decoding mental health issues experienced by individuals belonging to different communities
Decoding and spreading knowledge about techniques for emotional coping and mental health concerns
